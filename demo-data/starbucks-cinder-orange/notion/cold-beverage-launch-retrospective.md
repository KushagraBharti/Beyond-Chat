# Cold Beverage Launch Retrospective

Source type: Notion company knowledge

## Internal Readout

Prior cold beverage launches performed best when four conditions were true:

- The beverage looked visually distinct in app and drive-thru merchandising.
- The flavor hook was easy to explain in under five words.
- Store partners could make the product without slowing peak morning flow.
- The drink created a new seasonal reason to visit without replacing core cold brew.

## Patterns

- Cold coffee buyers respond to limited-time products when the base remains coffee-forward.
- Refreshers customers respond to color and fruit language, but cold brew customers need stronger coffee cues.
- Mobile app tile performance improves when the drink has color contrast and a clear seasonal ingredient.

## Launch Lessons

- Citrus language performs better when paired with peel, zest, or brightness rather than juice.
- A premium cold brew launch should avoid discounting in the first two weeks.
- The clearest callout is `bright citrus cold brew`, not `orange coffee`.

## Public Grounding

Starbucks has publicly noted that roughly two-thirds of U.S. company-operated coffeehouse beverage sales are cold and that cold beverages account for roughly 60% of international beverage sales.
